abstract: 'While large language models (LLMs) exhibit impressive performance on a wide range of NLP tasks, most of them fail to learn the causality from correlation. We first explain the underlying theoretical mechanism of their failure and argue that both data imbalance and the omission of causality in model design render the current paradigm failed to select causality-based models. We propose a causality-aware self-attention mechanism (CASAM) and eight kinds of legal-specific attacks for evaluation. Experimental results demonstrate CASAM achieves SOTA performances and the strongest robustness on three legal text prediction benchmarks.'
