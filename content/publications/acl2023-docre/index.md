abstract: 'Document-level relation extraction (DocRE) attracts more research interest recently. While models achieve consistent performance gains in DocRE, their underlying decision rules are still understudied. In this paper, we take the first step toward answering this question and then introduce a new perspective on comprehensively evaluating a model. We first conduct annotations to provide the rationales considered by humans in DocRE, then reveal that the representative SOTA models exhibit different decision rules from humans. Through RE-specific attacks, we demonstrate that the significant discrepancy severely damages robustness. We introduce MAP to evaluate understanding and reasoning capabilities of models.'
